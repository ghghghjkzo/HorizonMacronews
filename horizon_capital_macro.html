VIX — Regime Change Analysis

On March 2, 2026, the VIX closed at 22.40 (+12%), breaching the psychologically critical 20-level. This is not a single-day spike — it represents a structural regime shift away from the "Goldilocks" complacency that characterised early 2026.

Historical precedent: sustained VIX above 22 has preceded equity corrections of 3–5% on all but two occasions since 1990. Short-volatility strategies were forced to cover, amplifying the move. The MOVE Index simultaneously elevated — a rare double risk-off signal across equity and bond volatility.

Key threshold mapping: VIX above 30 = "high stress" scenario if oil sustains above $90/bbl. VIX 15–18 = normalisation, contingent on rapid Iran de-escalation and constructive Fed guidance.

Supply Disruption — Strait of Hormuz

The near-closure of the Strait of Hormuz represents a genuine physical supply disruption — not a risk-premium event. Approximately 20% of global oil and 15% of global LNG transits the strait daily. VLCC supertanker rates reached an all-time record (+94% in one session), with benchmark freight for Middle East–China routes rising to $423,736/day.

The structural oil backdrop remains bearish: the IEA projects a 3.85 Mbpd global surplus in 2026, OPEC+ paused production increases in Q1, and the EIA's pre-conflict baseline was Brent at $58/bbl for 2026. The geopolitical premium of $10–15/bbl is temporary absent sustained escalation.

Macro transmission: Each $10/bbl rise = +0.2pp US CPI, –0.1pp GDP growth. Sustained Brent above $90 for 2+ weeks would likely suspend the Fed's rate-cut cycle entirely.

Synthesis — Market Regime, March 2026

1. Physical supply shock — not merely a risk premium. The near-closure of the Strait of Hormuz represents a genuine disruption to 20% of global oil supply. The base case (Kpler) is a price spike followed by partial recovery to Brent $70–80 as OPEC spare capacity (~3.5 Mbpd) is activated over 3–6 weeks. A sustained closure — tail probability of 5% — implies $100–150/bbl with no adequate short-term offset available globally.

2. The dollar rebound is tactical noise. The DXY move to 98 will be sold. The structural bear case rests on three pillars that are not affected by the Iran conflict: the Fed's dovish cycle, Trump's explicitly weak-dollar policy, and the secular de-dollarisation of global reserve allocations. Institutional consensus: DXY 90–95 by year-end.

3. Gold has been permanently re-rated as a core institutional reserve asset. The convergence of central bank structural buying, debasement risk, and geopolitical demand creates a floor that did not exist in prior cycles. Consensus range for 2026: $4,500–$5,300. Stress scenario (JPM): $6,000+ if 0.5% of foreign USD holdings diversify into gold.

Suggested positioning in the current regime: Overweight gold, silver, JPY, CHF and defence equities. Underweight USD (sell rallies). Hedge equities via S&P 500 put options or VIX calls. Oil: neutral — the geopolitical spike is temporary, the fundamental overhang persistent. Bitcoin: neutral short-term, structurally constructive via the debasement trade.
